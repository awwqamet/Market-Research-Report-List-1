<p><ul><li>Splint</li><li>Support Strap</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The thumb braces market is primarily segmented into two types: splints and support straps. Splints provide rigid support and immobilization for the thumb, often used for injuries or conditions like arthritis. They are designed to stabilize the joint and promote healing. Support straps, on the other hand, offer adjustable compression and support, allowing for greater mobility while providing stability. These are commonly used for mild sprains or strains, making them suitable for everyday activities. Both types cater to varying degrees of support needs.</p></p>

<p><p>The thumb braces market is segmented into applications for stabilization and abduction. Stabilization braces are designed to support and immobilize the thumb, helping to alleviate pain and prevent further injury during rehabilitation. In contrast, abduction braces facilitate the controlled movement of the thumb away from the hand, enhancing the range of motion while ensuring proper alignment.
