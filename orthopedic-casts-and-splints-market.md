<p><p>The orthopedic casts and splints market is classified into two primary types: casts and splints. Casts are rigid immobilization devices, often made of plaster or fiberglass, used to stabilize broken bones and fractures during healing. In contrast, splints are flexible supports that can be adjusted and are used for minor injuries or conditions requiring temporary stabilization. Both products are essential in orthopedic care, facilitating recovery and preventing further injury by ensuring proper alignment and support for affected areas.</p></p>
