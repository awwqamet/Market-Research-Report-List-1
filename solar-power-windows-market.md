<p><p>The solar power windows market comprises several technologies. Crystalline silicon (c-Si) is the most common and efficient, utilizing silicon wafers for energy conversion. Organic Photovoltaics (OPV) are lightweight and flexible, but less efficient. Dye-Sensitized Solar Cells (DSSC) mimic photosynthesis, offering aesthetic integration. Cadmium Telluride (CdTe) is a thin-film technology known for lower costs, while amorphous silicon (a-Si) is also a thin-film option with flexibility. Other emerging technologies are in development, aiming for improved efficiency and affordability.</p></p>
